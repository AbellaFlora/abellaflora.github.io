Flowers

For many years our ancestors used them, not only to decorate, but also as an offering to their gods, as dyes, handicrafts, textiles, drinks, sculptures, textiles and even as medicine to cure diseases.

Cempasúchil


One of the most beloved native flowers of our country. Symbol of the Day of the Dead.Its name comes from Nahuatl and means flower with twenty petals.

Nochebuena


It means "fiery flower". It is native to southern Mexico and oral tradition says that it was the emperor Moctezuma who discovered it.The Spaniards named it Christmas Eve because it blooms in December.

Orquidea mexicana


Exotic and elegant, this epiphytic flower grows on the branches of trees, near the crown, where they receive good lighting and also the humidity they need.

Dalias


Dahlias were used to adorn pre-Hispanic temples and their petals were used to make natural dyes for cotton garments.This flower is used in traditional medicine and, in some regions, its petals and tuber are still cooked.

Magnolias


They are very white flowers, with a silky texture and a really intoxicating perfume. Its name in Nahuatl is yoloxóchitl, which means "heart flower".

Flor de arnica


The arnica flower was already highly appreciated in pre-Hispanic times for its medicinal qualities.This flower has anti-inflammatory properties.

Trees

Mexico is recognized for its biodiversity, ranking fourth in the world for its flora. Therefore, in our country there is a great variety of endemic trees.

Fungus

Fungus

Edible mushrooms are, with all certainty, one of the elements that provide originality and character to Mexican cuisine.

PAMBAZO

It is traditional from central Mexico and is great for making creams and sauces.It is a great side dish when we talk about red meats because the flavor is very marked.

HONGO AZUL

His blue tones are surprising.The flavor is very special to earth with sweet notes, what we recommend is that you enjoy it alone. It is traditional from Tlaxcala and grows wild.

CLAVITO

Several come attached at the bottom. The stem is white and changes color until the hat is brown.Its flavor is quite mild, so it goes very well with meats such as chicken and pork.

Insects

It is the leading country in the insectivorous revolution, with the largest variety of edible insects in the world: more than 300 species.

Acraga Coa


Acraga coa is a curious moth that can be found in Mexico (for example in Cancun), Belize, Honduras, Guatemala, El Salvador, Costa Rica and Panama. Even today, biologists aren't sure why these insect species are so colorful, but they suspect it's a defense mechanism to avoid predators, like so many other species.

Blue datalistragonfly


The blue dragonfly (Enallagma cyathigerum) can measure from 32 to 35 mm. Although it is common throughout Europe (except in Iceland), we can find it even in central Asia. The eyes of dragonflies are the largest and also one of the most precise of all the insects on the planet, as they can see 360º of their surroundings without having to turn their heads.

Twilight moth


Endemic to Madagascar, the twilight moth (Chrysiridia rhipheus) is a flying moth famous for its striking coloration. Very popular among collectors, this moth is considered one of the most impressive and attractive insects on the planet.

Chinese mantis


The Chinese mantis (Tenodera sinensis) is a species of mantis native to Asia. It is the largest mantis species that exists, being able to reach 11 centimeters in length. Like most mantises, it practices cannibalism and mantises have even been observed eating monarch butterflies after discarding the (toxic) entrails.

Rainbow grasshopper


The rainbow grasshopper or painted grasshopper (Dactylotum bicolor) is a curious species of arthropod that can be found in North America. As its name advances, it has a wide variety of colors distributed throughout its body such as blue, red, black, yellow or orange.

Bocydium globulare


Arguably the rarest insect on the planet. The Bocydium globulare lives in the trees of the Brazilian jungles and although its appearance surprises us, it only measures 4 mm. One of the most striking features of this insect is the morphology of its head that rises vertically with 4 small balls as protagonists, as if it were a curious sculpture.
